# Research

My research examines how digital information and analytics shape clinical work, operational performance, and equity in healthcare delivery. I focus on the informational consequences of EHR design and use, including documentation practices, measurement frequency, and data quality. My work combines econometrics, causal machine learning, and natural language processing with operationally interpretable constructs derived from EHR and audit log data.

## Stream 1: Health IT, measurement, and equity
I study how technology mediated measurement and documentation patterns generate disparities in process and outcome, and how these informational frictions propagate into readmissions, length of stay, and related performance metrics.

## Stream 2: Clinical workflows and collaboration in complex service systems
I develop measures of workflow, workload, and staff collaboration from audit logs and dynamic graphs to quantify operational frictions and identify levers for improving efficiency and quality of care.

## Stream 3: Human and AI collaboration for decision support
I study how human expertise and AI models can be integrated for complex information processing tasks in healthcare, including frameworks and methods that improve results to practice translation under task complexity.

## Broader interests
My research also examines the economics of information systems and digital platforms, including how information presentation and content structure influence user decision making.
